## 1. The Law of the Format (episode anatomy)

- **The scroll decision happens in under 2 seconds.** 70%+ of viewers leave in
  the first 2 seconds if not grabbed. The hook fires before the thumb moves.
- **Cold-open mid-crisis, always.** No titles, no recap, no greeting. "The slap
  has already landed. The gun has already jammed. The bride has already bolted."
  Conflict in the first LINE, not the first act. The soap-inherited variant:
  re-detonate the previous episode's cliff as the opening image.
- **Per-episode shape = compressed 3-act** (the "Beat Engine," ~500 words/90s):
  - **0–15s THE DETONATION** — straight into crisis, zero setup.
  - **15–60s THE ESCALATOR** — a *but* or *because* every other line; no
    connective tissue; stakes rise continuously.
  - **Final 5s THE FREEZE-FRAME CLIFF** — cut to black the instant it lands.
- **Write the ending first.** Decide the final 3 seconds (reveal / reversal /
  line that changes everything), then write backwards to set it up.
- **Micro-cliffhangers inside the episode:** 2–3 interrupted actions/suspended
  lines before the terminal cliff — "a density traditional TV would find
  comically aggressive."
- **The end frame is tomorrow's thumbnail.** End on an image an actor can hold.
  (ReelShort reportedly times freeze-frames to seconds 55–58 of 60.)
- **Every episode ends on a cliff. No exceptions. The story never resolves.**
  The psychology is named: the Zeigarnik effect — interrupted stories imprint.
- **The cliff must pose a SPECIFIC question** ("will she open the envelope?"),
  never general suspense.
- **Hook test:** describe the first 3 seconds to someone cold. If they ask a
  follow-up question, it works. If they shrug, rewrite.

## 2. Cliffhanger Taxonomy (rotate these — duanju.fr)

1. **Open question** — a direct unanswered dramatic question.
2. **Suspended decision** — character caught mid-choice.
3. **Deferred revelation** — the envelope/door/test result, unopened.
4. **Countdown** — shrinking time.
5. **Reversal** — a certainty upended in the final second.
6. **Dramatic irony** — audience learns what the hero doesn't know.
7. **Dilemma** — two bad options; "promises meaning more than action."

Ideal cliff = "unpredictable in its form, necessary in its meaning." Don't use
the same type twice in a row.

## 3. The Face-Slap Engine (打脸 — the atomic beat)

The single most reliable beat in the format. Structure:
1. Protagonist appears weak/poor/plain; **publicly humiliated** by a
   status-superior antagonist.
2. Antagonist **doubles down** — sneering disbelief, public mockery, a wager.
3. **The reveal** — hidden identity/wealth/power surfaces (the phone call, the
   CEO bowing, the will being read).
4. **THE REACTION IS THE PAYOFF.** Linger on the antagonist's shock, horror,
   grovel — NOT the protagonist's triumph. The camera stays on the villain's
   face cracking.
5. Repeat with escalating antagonists — each defeated one becomes loyal, an
   ally, or is replaced by a bigger one.

Supporting grammar: **爽点 (shuǎngdiǎn)** = instant-gratification pleasure
points stacked through the episode; **爆点 (bàodiǎn)** = detonation moments.
**Dramatic irony is oxygen**: the audience must know the secret (heir,
billionaire, mastermind) within the first 10–15 seconds of a series — the
pleasure is *anticipating* the face-slap, not being surprised by it.

**Beats are the product, not plots.** Producers repeat one proven element
("a woman bullied and slapped") verbatim across productions until engagement
decays. Catalog beats. A/B them. Transformation/mask-off reveals are engineered
to double as shareable clips — the trope IS the marketing asset.

## 4. Genre Templates & Highest-Converting Tropes

**The canon (ReelShort/DramaBox-proven):** hidden/secret billionaire, revenge
& comeback, contract/flash marriage, secret heir(ess), mistaken identity,
hidden pregnancy/secret baby, amnesia, rebirth/regression revenge, werewolf-
alpha (Western fantasy skin — see Africa notes: do NOT import unlocalized).

**Proof points:** *The Double Life of My Billionaire Husband* (450–500M+ views;
contract marriage + hidden billionaire + Cinderella abuse; the ep-1 hook is the
stepmother's slap). *Never Divorce a Secret Billionaire Heiress* (heroine used
as "a walking blood bank" by in-laws for 3 years → demands divorce → reveals
she's the billionaire).

**Highest-converting beat-level tropes** (all Nollywood-native — this is the
African advantage): **Bride-Price/Lobola Standoff** · **Toxic In-Laws** (public
fallout) · **Sibling Drain** (woman bled dry by relatives) · **Borrowed-Money
Betrayal** (loan vanishes; borrower returns rich) · **Auction Flex** (heroine
outbids the villain) · **Home-Buying Humiliation** (secret wire transfer flips
the room). "Tropes are load-bearing — you're borrowing trust the audience
learned from thousands of episodes."

**Audience:** women, decisively — 70% of ReelShort's MAU; women 35–54
over-index ~2×. The winning payoff blend is **cathartic revenge + hopeful
romance** — vindication AND love, never either alone. Radical simplicity
doctrine (ReelShort founder): simpler arcs than TV; "every second is a drama";
skip plausibility — nobody needs to know *why* the rich man loves the poor
girl.

## 5. Season Architecture (60–100 episodes)

- **Beat = episode.** The season beat-sheet and the episode grid are the same
  document. An 80-ep season = 80 hooks, 80 escalations, 80 cliffs.
- **Five-block blueprint:** eps 1–10 world/stakes + **2–3 emotional peaks
  before ep 10** (front-load; never coast early — ep 1 is the discovery
  surface for the whole series) · eps 11–30 major complications · eps 31–50
  reversals/betrayals/subplots converge · eps 51–70 acceleration, secrets out
  · eps 71–100 climax (resolution withheld as long as possible).
- **Macro-turns:** inciting reveal early · **false win ~1/3 mark** (victory
  that curdles) · **midpoint betrayal** · **lowest point** · payoff.
  Escalation wave every 5–10 episodes: reveal, reversal, new antagonist.
- **Clip-worthy moment every 2–4 episodes** (shareable set-pieces).
- On paid apps the hardest cliff sits on the last free episode (~ep 8–10) with
  the answering reveal 1–2 eps later; on free platforms this converts to:
  **place your hardest cliffs early and often — every episode is an entry
  point served cold to strangers.**

## 6. Dialogue & Captions

- **Short, punchy, conflict-driven lines.** Long monologues kill — the thumb
  moves. ONE exception: the confession/revelation at an episode-ending
  emotional peak may run long (viewer is locked in).
- **Caption-first writing:** 85%+ of social video plays silent. Subtitle lines
  **15–25 characters, max 2 lines**, break at natural speech rhythm. Captioned
  video: up to 40% more views, 80% higher completion.
- No exposition scenes ever — backstory woven into action. "A subplot that
  takes three TV episodes must work in three scenes."
- **The 9:16 frame carries meaning through hands, eyes and objects** more than
  dialogue. Write the insert shot.
- Put the biggest / most lip-sync-risky line **off-screen over a reaction**.

## 7. Africa: Markets, Registers, Language

**The moment:** Showmax shuts April 2026; DCAA is training 300 creators for
90-second vertical episodes; Both Worlds/Amazi shipped isiXhosa & isiZulu+
Sesotho verticals; LebaraPlay (telco) launched July 2026; Mansa shipping 10
microdramas across NG/KE/UG/SA. Nigeria is a **top-5 global download market**
for microdrama apps (ReelShort 300K+ WAU Q3 2024; DramaBox #2 entertainment
app in Kenya). Appetite precedes local supply — **no dominant African
vertical-drama channel exists yet on Shorts/TikTok.**

**The native mapping (localize the engine, not the dub):**
- Secret heir → **paternity secret / succession war** (SA's first festival
  microdrama is literally a co-wife/heir-paternity story).
- Hidden billionaire → **grass-to-grace / rich-disguised-as-poor** (already a
  Nigerian TikTok discovery category).
- Revenge → **muthi/juju payback**; contract marriage → **lobola economics**.
- Documented failure modes: hard paywalls (free/ad-funded is the cultural
  default) and **unlocalized fantasy skins (werewolves) that map to nothing**.

**Market registers (what detonates where):**
- **Nigeria:** romance-to-marriage warfare (whirlwind romance → pregnancy →
  rushed wedding → year-one warfare with money/exes/family — *Love in Every
  Word*, 31M views); Pentecostal morality & pastor plots (hero OR exposed
  fraud); ritual-wealth-always-destroys; street hustle & confraternity realism
  (*Selina Tested*, 1M+/ep, Pidgin-first). **Moral-resolution law: evil is
  ALWAYS eventually punished; the righteous vindicated.** The didactic loop is
  the renewable engine — violating it breaks the audience contract. (Within a
  never-ending serial: justice arrives per-arc, while the next sin opens.)
- **South Africa:** township family-crime-ancestor melodrama (Uzalo: twins
  separated at birth, church vs crime syndicate, 5.7M peak; muthi plots
  routine — the muthi-using schemer becomes the most-hated, most-watched
  character); **umjolo** (relationship betrayal — THE organizing TikTok genre
  keyword); **black tax vs lobola** — the hero's money contested between
  romantic future and family obligation, the sharpest SA-specific engine;
  ukuthwasa (the ancestral calling refused → misfortune) as a live modern
  tension, treated with respect-plus-fear.
- **Kenya:** grounded social-issue telenovela register (GBV, infertility,
  class — *Zora*; female-centered lens); rich-boy/poor-girl (*Maria*, 374
  eps); house-help vs boss class comedy; loyalty-slot viewing.

**Language IS characterization — flat English reads as translated:**
- **Nigeria:** English (formal) / **Pidgin** (street — "the most cinematic
  language in Nigeria") / Yoruba・Igbo (intimate). Switching signals register.
- **SA:** lead with a flagship vernacular the way Uzalo (isiZulu) and Skeem
  Saam (Sepedi) do; English is connective tissue; vernacular carries emotion,
  elders, ritual, insults.
- **Kenya:** English (class) / Swahili (public) / **Sheng** (peers, urban).
- In practice for the engine: dialogue mixes market-authentic names, honorifics,
  exclamations and code-switches; captions can carry the English.

## 8. Free-Platform Distribution Rules (YouTube Shorts + TikTok)

- **The paywall's replacement is the algorithm.** KPIs: YouTube "Viewed vs
  Swiped Away" ≥70–75%; binge-through (episodic content retains at **2.4× the
  rate of standalone posts** — TikTok data); loop endings (final frame
  reconnects to the first second — farms replays); comment-bait speculation
  (cliffs drive comments = ranking signal); "EP.1"/"Part 1" labeling + pinned
  ordering + playlists (YouTube now has Netflix-style season/episode playlists
  that include Shorts).
- **Monetization asymmetry (strategic):** YouTube = the wallet — AI content is
  YPP-eligible if "authentic" and varied (~$25–100/1M Shorts views); **TikTok
  Creator Rewards explicitly EXCLUDES fully-AI content** — TikTok is for
  discovery + its Mini Dramas commercial lane (launched June 2026).
- **AI-content survival rules:** label AI content on both platforms (YouTube:
  no algorithmic penalty; TikTok: mandatory, escalating bans). YouTube's
  "inauthentic content" policy killed 16 channels/4.7B views in one Jan 2026
  sweep; Mohan has declared war on "AI slop." Keep uploads **under ~10
  structurally-similar Shorts/day, 3–5/day/channel with real variation**.
  TikTok serialized cadence: **48–72h between episodes** recommended by TikTok
  for Business (rewatch loops + comment accumulation).
- **Precedent:** *Fruit Love Island* — fully-AI serialized fiction, free
  social-only, 300M+ views / 3M followers in ~2 weeks (March 2026). The model
  works. (Its death by format-theft controversy is the cautionary tale:
  originate, don't lift.)

## 9. AI Production Notes (2026 state)

- **Seedance 2.0** (Feb 2026): native synced dialogue/ambience/music,
  @Character tag system holding **~95% identity across 8–12 shots**, 15s
  clips — the dialogue/character workhorse. **Kling 3.0**: 4K/action/physics.
  **Veo 3.1**: premium hero shots. Best practice: **route by shot type**, not
  one model for everything.
- AI excels at spectacle and heightened melodrama; still lags on subtle
  emotional nuance → **lean into the format's strengths: big emotions, clear
  status shifts, reaction shots.** (The format wants melodrama; the tool wants
  melodrama.)
- Cost benchmarks: fully-AI verticals shipped at ~90% cost reduction, 6-week
  series (Vigloo); $12–14K/series (Dashverse); China ships ~470 AI titles/day.
  A 60s/4-shot episode engine is well inside the proven envelope.
